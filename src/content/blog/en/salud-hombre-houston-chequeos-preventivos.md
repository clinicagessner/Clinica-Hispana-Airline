# Men's Health in Houston: Preventive Checkups That Can Save Your Life

Many Hispanic men in Houston only see a doctor when the pain won't let them work anymore. The problem is that the most dangerous conditions — high blood pressure, diabetes, high cholesterol and prostate problems — progress silently for years without symptoms. By the time you feel them, the damage is already done.

The good news: a routine preventive checkup can catch these conditions early, when they are still easy to control. At Clínica Hispana Airline we do it in Spanish, with no appointment needed and affordable pricing.

## Why Should Men Get Regular Checkups?

The statistics are clear: men visit the doctor far less than women, so their conditions are found later. An annual checkup lets you:

- Detect high blood pressure, diabetes and high cholesterol before they damage your heart and kidneys
- Identify prostate changes at early, treatable stages
- Know your testosterone levels if you feel fatigued or low on energy
- Catch infections that show no symptoms
- Build a year-over-year baseline so any change stands out

A blood test and a general exam take less than an hour. That hour can add years to your life.

## Our Men's Health Services

### Prostate Exam (PSA)

The prostate-specific antigen (PSA) is a simple blood test that helps detect prostate problems, including cancer, at early stages. It's generally recommended to discuss this test starting at age 50 — or from 40-45 if prostate cancer runs in your family. Our [men's health](/services/salud-hombre) team explains your results clearly, in Spanish.

### Testosterone Panel

Feeling tired all the time, low on energy, moody or with low libido? Low testosterone is more common than people think after 40. A blood test measures your level and tells us whether you need treatment or further studies.

### Complete Blood Work

Our [blood testing lab](/services/examenes-sangre) measures glucose, cholesterol, triglycerides, kidney and liver function, among other key values. It's the most direct way to know how your body is doing on the inside.

### Electrocardiogram

Heart disease is the number one cause of death in men. An [electrocardiogram](/services/electrocardiograma) evaluates your heart's rhythm and electrical activity in minutes, painlessly.

### Chronic Condition Management

If you've already been diagnosed with diabetes, high blood pressure or high cholesterol, consistent follow-up is what prevents complications. Our [chronic condition management](/services/condiciones-cronicas) program includes monitoring, medication adjustments and a clear plan in your language.

## Conditions We Commonly Treat

### High Blood Pressure

Hypertension doesn't hurt, but it damages the heart, brain and kidneys. Nearly 1 in 2 adults has it — and many don't know. Checking it takes one minute.

### Type 2 Diabetes

The Hispanic community has a higher risk of developing diabetes. Excessive thirst, fatigue and frequent urination are late signs — early detection comes from a simple glucose test.

### Urinary and Prostate Infections

Burning when urinating, frequent urges or trouble getting started can point to a [urinary tract infection](/services/infecciones-urinarias) or an inflamed prostate. Both respond well to treatment when caught early.

### Sexually Transmitted Infections

Many STDs cause no symptoms in men but still do damage and spread to partners. We offer [confidential STD testing](/services/enfermedades-transmision-sexual) with fast results and respectful care.

## Recommended Exams by Age

| Age | Recommended exams |
|------|----------------------|
| 18-39 | Blood pressure every year, cholesterol every 4-6 years, glucose if overweight, STD testing if sexually active |
| 40-49 | All of the above + glucose every 3 years, PSA if there's family history, testosterone check if symptoms appear |
| 50-64 | Full annual checkup, PSA conversation with your doctor, electrocardiogram if you have risk factors |
| 65+ | Full annual checkup, PSA as medically indicated, yearly cardiovascular and kidney evaluation |

These ages are a general guide — your family history and lifestyle may move some exams earlier. During your visit we'll define together what applies to you.

## Warning Signs: Don't Ignore Them

Come to the clinic as soon as possible if you experience:

- Chest pain or pressure, or shortness of breath with exertion
- Difficulty urinating, burning, or blood in your urine
- Excessive thirst, unexplained weight loss or constant fatigue
- Frequent headaches with blurry vision
- Swelling in your legs or ankles
- Changes in libido or erectile dysfunction (it can signal a circulation problem)
- A lump or pain in the testicles

None of these signs go away on their own. Finding the cause early makes all the difference.

## Practical Tips to Protect Your Health

- Get a general checkup once a year, even if you feel fine
- Walk at least 30 minutes a day — no gym required
- Cut back on soda and fried food; drink more water
- If you smoke, get help quitting: it's the single highest-impact change
- Sleep 7-8 hours — poor sleep raises blood pressure and blood sugar
- Don't ignore stress: it affects your heart as much as diet does
